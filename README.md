# Kulls

A 1 to 4 player game written for the Commodore VIC-20 circa 1986.
The objective is for the players to act as a team and capture the
travelling (fish-shaped) home before being eaten themselves by the
(skull-shaped) Kulls.

- A player moves (or points) by pressing the appropriate key on a
  connected keyboard.
- If a controller-remapper has been installed and configured, then
  then each player may optionally use a joystick. Hence, the game could
  be played by some players using a keyboard and others using a joystick.
- Home is captured by a player pointing at it when in an adjacent cell.
- The home cannot be captured unless at least one dollar has been taken,
  followed by at least one bomb being exploded. In a multi-player game,
  these actions don't have to be performed by the same player.
- Any player who dies during a level will be resurrected at the start of
  the next level (assuming one of your fellow players captures the home).
- Players can move diagonally around corners and point at home, dollars
  and bombs diagonally. Other mobile objects cannot move diagonally.
- Exploding a bomb will banish any Kulls adjacent to you into the central
  Kull Banishment Room. Kulls are able to leave the room whenever the
  door is open.
- A telephone-looking object is a teleport.

## Brief history

Kulls was:

- Written by Kull circa 1986.
- Played and enjoyed from time to time for a decade or so until the VIC-20
  hardware failed.
- Transformed from a paper printout into a digital format then configured
  for the VICE emulator by MJ in April 2026.
- Released by me (grantj-re3) with the kind permission of Kull in May 2026.
